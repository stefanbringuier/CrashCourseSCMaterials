Basic Theory

  • As we saw in ?@fig-HgSC_HKO, there are materials where electrical conductivity drops to “exactly”1 zero.
  • How is this achieved?
    • Well at low-temperature we have Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (BCS) to thank Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957).
  • What mechanism did they describe?
    • Cooper Pairs!
    • Why Pairs? Blame the phonons.

BCS Theory: Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer theory of low-temperature superconductivity.

References

Bardeen, J., L. N. Cooper, and J. R. Schrieffer. 1957. “Theory of Superconductivity.” Phys. Rev. 108 (December): 1175–1204. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.108.1175.

Footnotes

  1. Here I use exactly in that it is zero within measurement precision. If your device can only measure to \(10^{-10}\) then you show a resistivity value on that order.↩︎
